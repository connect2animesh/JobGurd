How JobGuard Works

Detection methodology explained

🤖

ML Classification

A Logistic Regression model trained on labelled job postings predicts whether a posting is fake or real, outputting a confidence score alongside every analysis.

🔍

Pattern Matching

Over 60 regex patterns scan the job title and description for known scam phrases — fee requests, copy-paste work, ad-posting, urgency tactics, and MLM language.

📧

Email Domain Check

Legitimate companies always use a company-owned email. Personal domains like Gmail or Yahoo are flagged immediately as a strong red flag.

💰

Salary Sanity Check

Salaries that are too high per day or suspiciously below minimum wage are flagged automatically using numeric parsing of the salary field.

🔗

URL Validation

Shortened links (bit.ly, tinyurl) and suspicious TLDs (.xyz, .tk, .ml) commonly appear in phishing job posts and are flagged as high risk.

📊

Weighted Risk Score

Each red flag carries a severity weight (5–30 points). Scores are summed and combined with the ML probability: Final = 60% rules + 40% ML, capped at 100.

✅

Positive Signals

Mentions of PF/ESIC, CIN registration numbers, offer letters, structured interviews, background checks, and reputed portals reduce the overall risk score.

🛡

Safety Tips

Based on which flags are triggered, context-aware safety tips are shown — from verifying the company on MCA to never sharing Aadhar/PAN before an offer letter.

Risk Score Legend

0 – 25 ✅ Likely Safe No significant red flags detected.
26 – 55 ⚠️ Suspicious Proceed with caution — verify independently.
56 – 100 🚨 High Risk Multiple serious red flags — likely a scam.

⚠️ JobGuard is a heuristic + ML tool built for educational purposes. Always independently verify job postings before making any career decisions.
